Earth Sciences and Engineering

Nanoparticles in Wastewater Treatment

Advisor: Linda Y. Tseng

Analyzed water quality data from two wastewater treatment plants in Southern California to determine trends in biogenic nanoparticle behavior at different stages of treatment. Compared findings against existing literature to assess ways to maximize treatment plant efficiency.

Sustainable Development

Development Alternatives (DA) is a social enterprise dedicated to sustainable development. I worked on two projects:

Analysed existing marketing models and distribution channels suggested by researchers at Yale University to be used for Aqua+, a chlorine-based solution for water purification, tested these models for feasibility in the slums of Delhi and created numerical simulations to demonstrate profitability under different pricing schemes, suggesting refinements in the model for each case

Tested the capacity and efficiency of the flexipump, a low cost hand pump designed by UK-based Hutton Technologies for use by small farmers in developing nations, and developed a training toolkit and intensive testing protocol for further testing of the pump at partner farms across India
